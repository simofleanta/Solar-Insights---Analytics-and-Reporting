# Solar Insights — Energy Analytics

Energy analytics & reporting for solar (PV) producers, energy managers, and investors.
Live: **https://solarinsights.vercel.app**

This site presents a real, data-driven case study: **how the timing of solar
production interacts with wholesale electricity prices** on the Romanian
day-ahead market (DAM / PZU, operated by OPCOM).

---

## Featured analysis — Solar Output vs. Day-Ahead Prices

### The question
A solar park earns money by selling energy on the day-ahead market, where the
price changes **every hour**. Solar output is not constant either — it peaks at
midday. So the real question is not *how much* a park produces, but **what price
it captures for the energy it actually delivers.**

### The data
Hourly OPCOM market records for PV parks:

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Period | 1–22 June 2025 (22 days) |
| Granularity | Hourly, daylight hours (06:00–19:00) |
| Scope | 10 solar parks across 6 grid zones (Dobrogea, Muntenia, Oltenia, Moldova, Banat, Transilvania) |
| Key columns | energy delivered (kWh), day-ahead price (RON/MWh), revenue (RON), capacity factor |

### Method
- **Baseload price** — the simple average day-ahead price (≈ 287 RON/MWh).
- **VWAP (volume-weighted average price)** — the price actually captured by PV,
  weighted by the energy delivered in each hour: `Σ(price × energy) / Σ energy`.
- **Capture Rate** — `VWAP / baseload`. Below 100% means PV captures *less* than
  the market average, because it delivers most of its energy in cheap hours.
- **Hourly price index** — each hour's price relative to the daily average
  (`> 100` = expensive hour, `< 100` = cheap hour).
- **Storage shifting** — a simple battery-arbitrage model: move part of the
  midday (cheap) energy to the evening (expensive) hours, net of round-trip
  efficiency losses.

### Key findings
- **Capture Rate ≈ 96%** — PV consistently captures a price *below* the market
  average.
- **Production peaks at 13:00**, exactly where the day-ahead price bottoms out
  (≈ 268 RON/MWh), while prices peak in the morning (07:00 ≈ 304) and evening
  (19:00 ≈ 300) when PV barely produces.
- **Price timing alone cuts PV revenue by ~4%** versus the market average — a
  pure timing effect, not a technical loss.
- **Battery arbitrage was *not* profitable on this data**: the cheap-to-expensive
  price spread (~9%) was smaller than the battery's round-trip loss (~10%), so
  shifting energy actually lost money. Storage only pays when the price spread
  exceeds the efficiency loss.

### Interactive chart
The case-study section embeds an interactive Plotly chart
(`public/pzu-profil.html`) showing hourly energy delivered (bars) against the
day-ahead price (line) — the classic "scissor" shape of price cannibalisation.

## Tech stack
- **Website** — Next.js 14 (App Router) + Tailwind CSS, deployed on Vercel.
- **Analysis** — Python (pandas) over hourly OPCOM data in SQLite; metrics and
  charts generated programmatically.
- **Visualisation** — Plotly (interactive HTML, embedded via iframe).

## Local development
```bash
npm install
npm run dev      # http://localhost:3000
npm run build    # production build
```

The interactive chart in `public/pzu-profil.html` is generated by the analysis
script and dropped into `public/`; the site simply serves it.
